Beyond Technology

Endurance, Mindset, and Life Outside the Datacenter

Technology may be my profession, but endurance sports — especially marathon running — have shaped how I think about discipline, resilience, and long-term performance.

The Marathon Mindset

I’ve been running marathons since 2003 and have completed about 15 marathons to date — along with numerous 10Ks and 5Ks. Endurance has become more than a sport for me; it’s a mindset.

Marathon training teaches patience, structure, and consistency. You don’t get results from one great workout — you get them from showing up again and again with a long-term mindset. That same philosophy drives how I help organizations adopt AI and improve security operations: progress through repeatable systems, not one-off bursts.

Running has reinforced the importance of pacing, recovery, and adaptability — lessons that apply just as much to complex cloud systems and security programs as they do to the road.

Keynote

Endurance Mindset: Building Resilience for High-Stakes Work

What marathon training teaches us about sustainable performance, pressure, and long-term success.

In endurance sports, results don’t come from one great day — they come from consistency, pacing, and resilience through setbacks. The same principles apply to demanding careers, leadership roles, and high-pressure environments.

This keynote translates lessons from marathon running into a practical framework for building resilience at work and in life — without burnout, guilt cycles, or unsustainable intensity.

Key Themes

  • Consistency beats intensity
  • Pacing as a performance strategy
  • Recovery as a competitive advantage
  • Resetting quickly after setbacks
  • Building identity around finishing, not perfection
